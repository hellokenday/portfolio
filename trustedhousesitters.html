Introduction

TrustedHousesitters is a house and pet sitting platform for people to look after pets in exchange for a place to stay. Home and pet owners open their homes to pet sitters who want to travel and live like locals. The product can be accessed from its website or mobile app for iOS and Android.

The new web experience is currently being developed as an MVP and is being distributed to sample groups for testing before its full release.

My Role

I joined the company as the first product designer to work on redesigning the web experience. This involved usability evaluation of the product to gather design requirements, creating a design system, working in design sprints to validate ideas before moving into agile sprints. Later the team grew to include our UX researcher and UI designer who helped refine our design processes and continue improving the product.

Problem areas

Usability testing of the website revealed that first-time visitors did not understand the service, which we assumed was affecting conversion rates/membership sales.

Several problem areas were uncovered:

  1. No understanding of how the service works
    • participants did not have enough information before signing up
  2. Confusion with the membership pricing model
    • participants thought that sitters get paid to house sit
  3. No sense of trust
    • participants didn't feel comfortable letting strangers into their homes

From these problem areas, we created how might we questions and solutions for them. We then formed hypothesises to eliminate assumptions and ran tests to see if they would be valid.

Improve understanding of how the service works

Question

How might we get across how our service works before people go to sign-up?

Solution

Including information pieces on the sign-up modal

Hypothesis

  • We believe that including information pieces on the sign-up modal
  • For first-time visitors
  • Will get across how our service works before we prompt people to sign-up

Testing hypothesis

We ran usability tests to include information pieces on the sign-up modal and asked follow-up questions to identify the user's understanding of how the service works. The majority of participants understood that sitters petsit for free in exchange for a place to stay which validated the hypothesis.

Impact

The sign-up modal redesign was applied to the existing site which resulted in a 12.1% increase in sign-up page value.

Increase trust in our service

Question

How might we ensure people feel they can trust our service and be comfortable letting strangers into their homes?

Solution

Directing users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct

Hypothesis

  • We believe that directing users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct
  • For first-time visitors
  • Will ensure people feel they can trust our service and be comfortable letting strangers into their homes

Testing hypothesis

We ran usability tests to direct users to information about our safety verifications and code of conduct. We then asked follow-up questions to identify the user's emotion associated with our service (trustworthy, safe, legit). The majority of participants identified our service as safe or trustworthy and they would be willing to become a member. However, some participants were still hesitant about letting strangers into their home which is an ongoing problem we are solving.

Sign up & checkout

When redesigning the sign-up and checkout, we used a shortened 3-day design sprint which involved researching problems with the existing user flow, sketching ideas, prototyping, and remote user testing.

Goals

  • Improve ease of checkout
  • Ensure users understand the membership they are purchasing
  • Allow users to change membership part way through checkout
  • Allow users to change currency before payment

Results

Overall the checkout redesign performed fantastically on six testers, who all described it as being very easy to complete.

  • 4/6 testers were able to identify the details of a membership before purchasing (sign up free, yearly fee, no cost after)
  • 6/6 were able to change their currency either before or during checkout easily
  • 6/6 could recall the details of their membership after checkout

Impact

Core elements of the sign-up and checkout redesign were applied to the existing site which resulted in a 7.4% increase in checkout/billing page value.

Sitter onboarding

We ran a five-day design sprint to target our problem with member success rates.

Problem

A percentage of new sitters don't succeed in getting a housesit in their first three months of becoming a member which leads to refunds or discontinued memberships if they don't succeed at all.

Goals

  • Reduce refunds
  • Increase member retention
  • Long term goal: increase new sitter success within their first three months of becoming a member

Questions

  • How might we ensure users fill in their profiles to an adequate level?
  • How might we ensure users get verified?
  • How might we break up onboarding into clear steps?

Solution

Help new sitters fill out the essential parts of their profile and guide them on the kind of information homeowners would expect to see in prospective house sitters.

Testing

We tested the prototype in-house on six new members where it performed very well and was described as being an easy process. Most of the design recommendations were around the copy to ensure new members understood our safety verifications.

Design system

Creating a design system helped us reduce design debt, maintain consistency across designs, and speed up our process allowing us to iterate much faster.

The system is made up of UI components, layout guidelines, typography rules, colour usage, and navigation specs. Now the foundations are set, research into micro-interactions is underway.
