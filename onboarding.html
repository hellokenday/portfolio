Trusted Housesitters

iOS app user onboarding

Introduction

TrustedHousesitters is a house and pet sitting platform for people to look after pets in exchange for a place to stay.

The iOS app user onboarding helps new pet sitters create quality profiles to ensure enough information is included for them to succeed in securing their first house sit.

My role

As a designer on the sprint team, I focused on the prototype design as well as facilitating some of the usability testing and user interviews.

Problem

A percentage of new sitters don't succeed in getting a housesit in their first three months of becoming a member which leads to refunds or discontinued memberships if they don't succeed at all.

Goal

Increase pet sitter success ensuring new members get a house sit in their first three months of signing up to the service.

Success metrics

Increase new pet sitters success over the next six months (>10%).

Process

We decided to run a Google-style design sprint to come up with validated solutions.

The Design Sprint is a process for solving problems through research, designing, prototyping and testing ideas with users.

The process follows six phases: Understand, Define, Sketch, Decide, Prototype, and Validate.

The sprint

We met in a space for 5 consecutive days from Monday to Friday to carry out the workshop.
