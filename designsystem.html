TrustedHousesitters

Introduction

TrustedHousesitters is a house and pet sitting platform for people to look after pets in exchange for a place to stay.

With the redesign of the website, two mobile apps in the pipeline, and a rapidly growing team, it became increasingly important to unite teams and products around a common visual language.

My role

I was hired as the first product designer to create the design system foundations which involved:

  • Defining design principles
  • Auditing interface patterns
  • Establishing a pattern library
  • Creating master design file
  • Documenting patterns and guidelines on how to use them

Problem

After conducting stakeholder interviews and expert reviews, it was clear that the platform was inconsistent and resembled a patchwork experience.

Problem areas

  • Multiple design patterns solving the same problem
  • Design and technical debt
  • Slow process for creating new designs
  • Inconsistent styles weakening the brand

Goals

  • Increase design and engineering velocity
  • Unify the brand and overall experience across all platforms
  • Enable the product to scale whilst reducing design and technical debt

Solution

The main solution was to create a design system and document our new visual language.

A design system is a collection of reusable components, guided by clear standards, that can be assembled together to build any number of applications.
